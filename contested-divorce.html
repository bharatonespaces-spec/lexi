Overview

When a marriage breaks down and one partner is unwilling to separate, or parties cannot agree on terms, a Contested Divorce becomes necessary. These cases require a lawyer who is not only knowledgeable in law but skilled in strategy and evidence.


We handle cases on all grounds available under Hindu Marriage Act and Special Marriage Act, including Cruelty, Desertion, Adultery, and Conversion. Our focus is on building a watertight case to secure the relief you deserve.

Common Grounds for Divorce

Cruelty (Mental or Physical)

Conduct that causes reasonable apprehension in the mind of the petitioner that it is harmful to live with the respondent.

Desertion

Willful abandonment by the spouse for a continuous period of at least two years.

Adultery

Voluntary sexual intercourse with any person other than his or her spouse.

Legal Process

  • 1. Filing of Petition with specific allegations and evidence.
  • 2. Service of Summons to the other party.
  • 3. Counseling/Mediation (Mandatory in Family Courts).
  • 4. Evidence Stage (Examination in Chief & Cross Examination).
  • 5. Final Arguments & Judgment.
