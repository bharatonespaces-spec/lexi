5 Myths About Mutual Consent Divorce in India

December 14, 2024 | Divorce Law

Mutual Consent Divorce is the fastest and most dignified way to end a marriage in India. However, misinformation often causes unnecessary fear and delay. Let's bust the top 5 myths.

Myth 1: It Takes Years to Complete

Fact: Unlike contested divorces which can drag on for years, a Mutual Consent Divorce typically concludes in 6-8 months. In genuine hardship cases, the Supreme Court has even waived the mandatory 6-month cooling period.

Myth 2: Reasons Must Be Proved in Court

Fact: The beauty of Section 13B is that you do not need to wash dirty linen in public. You simply need to state that you have been living separately for over a year and have mutually agreed to part ways. No specific allegations of cruelty or adultery are required.

Myth 3: Alimony is Mandatory for the Husband

Fact: Alimony (maintenance) is purely based on mutual understanding. If the wife is earning well and the couple agrees to a "no-claim" settlement, the court will accept it. There is no fixed formula.

Myth 4: Child Custody Always Goes to the Mother

Fact: In mutual consent, parents decide the custody terms. It can be shared custody, weekend visitation, or exclusive custody to the father if agreed upon. The court's only concern is the welfare of the child.

Myth 5: You Can't Remarry Immediately

Fact: Once the final decree is signed by the judge, the marriage is legally dissolved. You are free to remarry immediately, unlike in contested cases where there is an appeal period.

Consultation Tip: Before filing, ensure all terms regarding assets, jewelry, and custody are written down in a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU). This avoids last-minute withdrawals.

Conclusion

Choosing Mutual Consent saves time, money, and emotional health. If you and your spouse have decided to move on, do it with dignity.
