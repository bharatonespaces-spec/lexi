Overview

Domestic violence is not just physical; it includes verbal, emotional, and economic abuse. The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 (PWDVA) is a powerful tool designed to provide rapid relief to victims.


We assist women in filing Domestic Incident Reports (DIR) and obtaining court orders that prevent the abuser from contacting them, disturbing their possession of the household, or selling assets.

Types of Reliefs Available

Protection Orders

Judicial orders restraining the respondent from committing violence, entering the workplace, or communicating with the victim.

Residence Orders

Ensuring the victim cannot be evicted from the shared household, regardless of whether she owns it.

Maintenance & Compensation

Monetary orders to cover medical expenses, loss of earnings, and maintenance for the victim and children.
