Why You Must Register a Will (Even if You Have Nominees)

December 14, 2024 | Property Law

A common misconception in India is that appointing a "Nominee" in your bank account or flat is enough to pass on ownership. This is legally incorrect and often leads to bitter family disputes.

Nominee is NOT the Owner

The Legal Reality: According to Indian succession laws, a nominee is merely a trustee or a custodian. Their job is to receive the asset from the bank or society and hold it until the legal heirs claim it.

If you have named your son as a nominee but your Will (or succession law) says the property should go to your daughter, the daughter is the true owner. The son must hand it over.

Why a Registered Will is Essential

Drafting a Will is the only way to dictate exactly who gets what. While an unregistered Will is valid, a Registered Will carries significantly more weight in court because:

  • It proves that you appeared before a government officer (Sub-Registrar).
  • It minimizes the chances of someone claiming the Will is fake or forged.
  • It is kept in safe custody with the Registrar's office.

The Risk of Intestacy (No Will)

If you pass away without a Will, your assets are distributed according to religious personal laws (e.g., Hindu Succession Act). This often creates fragmentation of property among many relatives, leading to complicated ownership issues that make selling the property nearly impossible.

Did You Know? Video-recording the signing of your Will acts as powerful supplementary evidence to prove that you were of sound mind and not under pressure.

Conclusion

Don't leave your family's future to chance or complex laws. A registered Will is the ultimate act of care, ensuring your assets become a blessing, not a burden.
